In Simple Words

What does the company actually do?

Think of Laser Power & Infra as a company that makes the "wires and cables" that carry electricity — power cables, conductors, control & instrumentation cables — under the brand “LASER.”

On top of making these products, it also takes up full projects (EPC) — meaning it designs, buys materials and builds the electricity network for towns and villages: laying lines, setting up sub-stations and electrifying rural areas end-to-end.

So it earns money in two ways: (1) selling cables & conductors, and (2) executing turnkey power-infrastructure projects for government utilities & discoms.

The Bottom Line

Should You Apply?

✅ APPLY — for listing gains + long term

Verdict: Subscribe (moderate conviction)

The issue is reasonably priced (post-issue P/E ~19.8x vs larger peers), profits are growing fast (PAT +42%), return ratios are strong (ROE ~23%), and a ₹3,243 Cr order book gives revenue visibility. Using the money to slash debt should lift future earnings. A firming GMP (~17%) and a Subscribe tilt from most brokers (8 Subscribe / 1 May Apply / 2 Neutral) support applying.

👍 Why YES (Strengths)

  • PAT up ~42% YoY; ~3.75x growth over 2 years
  • Strong return ratios: ROE 23.3%, RoNW 20.9%
  • Reasonable valuation vs larger listed peers
  • ₹3,243 Cr order book = revenue visibility
  • ₹490 Cr debt repayment → lower interest cost
  • Leader in East India; backward-integrated, 37-yr track record
  • Healthy GMP (~11%) & broad broker "Subscribe"

👎 Why to be CAUTIOUS (Risks)

  • FY26 revenue fell ~9% YoY — growth not linear
  • High leverage: Debt/Equity 1.10 (even post-issue)
  • Thin PAT margin (~6.5%) — EPC is competitive
  • Customer concentration (govt utilities/discoms)
  • Execution & raw-material (aluminium/copper) price risk
  • Lukewarm subscription so far (~1.0x by Day 2)
  • Issue looks "fully priced" per some analysts
Short Term

Decent listing pop likely if GMP (~17%) holds. Apply for listing gains, but keep expectations measured — modest overall subscription means gains aren't guaranteed. Book profits on a strong open if you're a trader.

Long Term

The better fit. Post-issue de-leveraging + strong order book + India's power capex cycle can compound earnings. Suits investors comfortable holding through EPC/commodity cycles. Dilip Davda: "park funds for medium to long term."
